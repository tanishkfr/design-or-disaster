## Question before the court

**What does a design judgment select as evidence before it becomes a verdict?**

Design or Disaster is not a quiz about whether an interface is good. It is an archive of incompatible perception.

## The jury

The panel is fallible and explicitly authored. Agreement can occur, but agreement is not accuracy. No crowd distribution, live telemetry, or correctness percentage is shown.

The Design Eye record reflects which evidence lenses the visitor repeatedly used and which stayed least visible. It is a trace of selection, not a grade against authority.

## Findings entered into the record

- Judgment becomes inspectable when evidence must be spatially located before a verdict.
- One screen can sustain several coherent but incompatible accounts.
- A blind spot is not simply a wrong answer; it is evidence that never entered a personâ€™s plate.
- The panel is most useful as structured disagreement, not institutional authority.

## Limits and disclosure

The jurors, cases, annotations, and screenshots are authored composites designed to stage critical positions. They are not a survey of experts or live participant data. The record remains in browser-local storage. The project demonstrates an interaction for examining perception; it does not establish population-level judgment patterns.

## Ruling

Design or Disaster contributes the **evidence plate** as an interaction grammar for critique: mark first, argue second, compare perception, then live with the ruling.

The exhibition image to remember is five transparent plates laid over one interface, each proving that attention has its own geometry.
